The Situation

Academic writing, unlike regular business writing or blogging is characterised by a few features that are not usually employed in other forms of writing. These include:

  1. Writing in paragraphs and using lists and bullet points, and numbers
  2. All facts must be referenced. So, we need to add references and citations
  3. Figures and tables in a number of different formats
  4. Formulae and codes
  5. Footnotes and endnotes

Kieran Healy (Healy 2011) wrote a blog post outlining the workflow that would be useful for most situations. A lot of papers are written in Word with Endnote for support and these work well. However, with the emergence of lightweight robust writing platforms that integrate the web and the scholarly literature, there is a space for other tools that are platform independent and useful for collaborative scholarly paper authoring. In this paper, let's explore some of the possibilities.
